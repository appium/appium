What is the Tomatometer?

The Tomatometer rating  based on the published opinions of hundreds of film and television critics  is a trusted measurement of movie and TV programming quality for millions of moviegoers. It represents the percentage of professional critic reviews that are positive for a given film or television show.

From the Critics

From RT Users Like You!

Fresh

The Tomatometer is 60% or higher.

Rotten

The Tomatometer is 59% or lower.

Certified Fresh

Movies and TV shows are Certified Fresh with a steady Tomatometer of 75% or higher after a set amount of reviews (80 for wide-release movies, 40 for limited-release movies, 20 for TV shows), including 5 reviews from Top Critics.

Audience Score

Percentage of users who rate a movie or TV show positively.

    ½

    Spectre takes the Craig era of Bond way back to the roots of what made the series famous: high octane action, crazy characters, and bloated plots that are fun to watch, but fall to scrutiny when you actually think about them. Daniel Craig is still as good as ever and Sam Mendes direction is terrific too, but Spectre is a little bloated and the plot tries to hard to tie together the entire story of the Craig era. Spectre was a highly enjoyable entry of the series, but did nothing to push the series forward like Skyfall or Casino Royale. This is pure 007 formula, but nonetheless worthwhile for a Bond fan.

    Josh Lewis

    Super Reviewer

    The newest entry in the James Bond franchise, "Spectre," represents everything great and average about all the films of the past. Relying on old characters of the Bond franchise in order to drive this film, I felt that took away from the central plot. Once James Bond is let go by his agency and placed on watch, he goes rogue to accomplish all of the side missions related to his past. Uncovering hidden secrets hidden throughout all of the other Daniel Craig films in this series, you will either buy it or you won't. It's not that I didn't enjoy that aspect, it's just that it felt easy to do and I wasn't emotionally connected to it at all. These films are more emotional than where this franchise first started, so that is what I look forward too going in. This film is filled with nostalgia, and for the most part, it all works well. Christophe Waltz and Dave Bautista are the two main focusses as the villains and I must say, they are very good in their roles, although Christophe Waltz felt a little underused. Although I have many complaints, this is a very fun James Bond flick with incredible cinematography, fun action scenes, and great character development. Sam Mendes back at the helm of director was a great choice, although he did have a lot of pressure on him this time around. In the end, it is not one of the better Bond films, but it is a harmless entry that you can have a blast watching. They tried very hard, and I will give them tons of props for that. I recommend "Spectre" to fans of the franchise, but not so much the newcomers, as this film does rely heavily on the past.

    KJ Proulx

    Super Reviewer

    ½

    Spectre incorporates a lot of references from the past that sort of provide a unifying whole to the previous four Bond entries. Recurring characters M, Q and Miss Moneypenny all return. New addition Léa Seydoux as Dr. Madeleine Swann is an attractive presence, although too judgmental for a "Bond girl". Spectre is the villanous global criminal network that forms the crux of the saga. Meanwhile the essential fate of the 007 organization is in jeopardy when a smug bureaucrat named C (Andrew Scott) takes over.British intelligence. The pinnacle of evil is Christoph Waltz, whom Bond tracks down to his desert lair housed in a meteor crater . He's a serviceable villain, but veers on the dull side. This is where you're supposed to ham it up, Christoph! His head drilling machine is kind of nasty though. I cringed at the scene.

    The Daniel Craig Bond era has been unusually strong. Over the course of four movies, the franchise has seen some of its most popular films. The series hit a zenith with Skyfall, an all around success by any standard. Quantum of Solace was execrable, but hey, three out of four ain't bad. Spectre isn't the best entry, but it's good entertaining fun nonetheless. Daniel Craig has intimated this may be the last time he plays Bond. If that's true, I will lament his decision. He's been one of my favorites.
